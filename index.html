About

I have words.
Not one for
everyone.

I have words — not one for everyone, but one for you. I'm very picky, so I'll ask you about everything: your story, your life, what you eat, what you're aiming for. You provide the ingredients. I cook the story.

I'm Sumiya. Pakistani writer and marketer, now in Bournemouth. At 20 I built a content business in Karachi. At 25 I landed in England alone, no scholarship, no family at arrivals — just a suitcase and the kind of certainty that only exists before reality shows up. I managed a fish and chips shop while finishing my Masters. Now I run HubSpot for a UK EdTech company. I've written mystery thrillers, email sequences, LinkedIn posts, and essays that made strangers message me at midnight.

I won't write anything I'd be embarrassed to put my name on. No filler that's only there because the brief said 800 words. No "thought leadership" with no actual thoughts in it. No copy that sounds like it was written by a committee trying not to offend anyone. If you want safe, I am the wrong hire. If you want something that sounds like a specific human wrote it — with a point of view and everything — we should talk.

I write about: Career, immigration, the gap between the CV and the true story, HubSpot, marketing, what it actually costs to start over. I don't write: Anything vague enough to be true of everyone.

Services

What I do
and how.

Pick the thing you need. If you're not sure which, email me and I'll tell you.

01

Blog & Long-Form Articles

I only write for two audiences: Google and the human being who's tired of reading articles that say nothing. You know that blog post you read at 2am that made you feel seen? That's what I'm making for your brand.

02

LinkedIn Content

A personal brand with no presence is never your problem when you're with me. I write for the people you actually want in your corner — the ones who'll read one post and think: I need to know this person. Then the opportunities follow.

03

Email Newsletters

Most newsletters talk at you. They have an agenda and a word count and they don't really care if you're there. Mine feel like someone pulled up a chair. You open them because you want to know what happens next — not because you forgot to unsubscribe.

04

Brand Storytelling

I won't wrap your story in a neat bow and call it a redemption arc. Real brands are still in the middle of the story — and the middle is where people actually connect. Give me the mess. I'll find the line that makes it matter.

05

HubSpot & MarTech Content

13 HubSpot certifications. I manage four brand instances daily. I write about this the way someone writes about a place they live in — not a place they looked up. The detail is different when you've actually been inside.

06

Ghostwriting

Before I write a word in your voice, I need to find one thing: the sentence you'd say at a dinner table, not a boardroom. That's where your actual voice lives. Once I have that, everything else follows. 5.0 on Upwork across 8 jobs. Your name on it, always.
